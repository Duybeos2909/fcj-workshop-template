> This section presents my personal reflections, evaluations, and suggestions after completing the internship at **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)**. I hope these comments can contribute to improving the program for future internship cohorts.

## Overall Evaluation

### 1. Internship Environment

The learning environment at **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)** is open, dynamic, and encourages self-learning. Throughout the internship, I had the opportunity to explore a wide range of AWS services and work on practical cloud-related tasks rather than simply following theoretical tutorials.

The community communication channels were always active, making it easy for interns to exchange ideas, discuss technical issues, and support one another during labs and project implementation. This collaborative atmosphere greatly motivated me to become more proactive in learning and problem-solving.

### 2. Support from Mentors and Admin Team

The mentors played the role of guides instead of simply providing answers. Whenever I encountered problems related to AWS services or system deployment, they encouraged me to analyze the root cause, investigate CloudWatch logs, and gradually identify appropriate solutions on my own.

In addition, the admin team continuously updated important announcements and organized Community Day events, workshops, and technical sharing sessions. These activities provided valuable opportunities to learn from experienced engineers and industry professionals.

### 3. Relevance to My Academic Major

The internship program was highly relevant to my university major, especially in the areas of **Cloud Computing, AWS Serverless Architecture, and DevOps**.

By designing the architecture of the **Smart Attendance SaaS Platform**, writing technical blogs, developing workshop documentation, and studying AWS services, I was able to apply theoretical knowledge from university to practical cloud engineering scenarios while gaining a deeper understanding of modern software deployment processes.

### 4. Learning Opportunities and Skill Development

This was the aspect I appreciated the most during the internship.

Besides learning numerous AWS services, I also strengthened many important professional skills, including:

- Designing cloud architectures on AWS.
- Writing technical documentation and workshop guides.
- Creating technical blog posts.
- Presenting ideas and reporting work progress.
- Collaborating effectively within a team.
- Researching official AWS documentation to solve technical problems independently.

Participating in Community Day events also broadened my understanding of current technology trends, Artificial Intelligence, Cloud Computing, and career development within the IT industry.

### 5. Community Culture

One of the strongest aspects of FCAJ is its knowledge-sharing culture.

Members were always willing to help each other whenever someone encountered deployment issues or difficulties while completing AWS labs. This supportive environment made me more confident in asking questions, sharing ideas, and learning collaboratively with other interns.

### 6. Learning Resources and Support

The program provided comprehensive learning materials, technical workshops, and AWS Sandbox environments that allowed interns to practice without worrying about cloud service costs.

This gave me the opportunity to gain hands-on experience with many AWS services such as Amazon EC2, Amazon S3, Amazon CloudFront, Amazon API Gateway, AWS Lambda, Amazon DynamoDB, Amazon Cognito, and many other cloud services in an environment similar to real-world production systems.

---

## Survey Responses

### What I Was Most Satisfied With

The most valuable part of the internship was having direct access to AWS services through hands-on labs, workshops, and practical assignments. Designing cloud architectures, studying AWS services, and writing technical documentation significantly improved both my technical knowledge and my confidence in working with cloud technologies.

### Areas for Improvement

I suggest adding individual **One-on-One Review** sessions during the middle of the internship. These meetings would allow mentors to evaluate each intern's progress, identify strengths and weaknesses, and provide personalized guidance before the final project stage.

### Would I Recommend This Program?

**Yes.**

I would definitely recommend the **First Cloud AI Journey** internship program to students who are interested in Cloud Computing, AWS, or DevOps. The program provides valuable practical experience, a supportive learning community, and a solid foundation for future careers in cloud engineering.

---

## Suggestions and Future Expectations

To further improve the internship program, I would like to suggest the following:

- **AWS Certification Roadmap:** Provide learning resources or mentoring sessions for internationally recognized AWS certifications such as **AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner** and **AWS Certified Solutions Architect – Associate**, helping students prepare for professional certifications after completing the internship.

- **Cross-Team Technical Sharing Sessions:** Organize short technical presentations where different project teams introduce their solution architectures and design decisions. This would allow interns to learn multiple approaches for solving similar cloud engineering problems.

- **Advanced Infrastructure as Code (IaC) Content:** Besides AWS SAM, include additional workshops or reference materials covering **Terraform**, **AWS CloudFormation**, and **AWS CDK** to better prepare students for modern DevOps and Cloud Engineering roles.

Overall, the internship at **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)** provided me with valuable technical knowledge, practical experience, and many opportunities for personal and professional growth. It strengthened my understanding of Cloud Computing, enhanced my technical skills, and established a solid foundation for pursuing a future career in AWS Cloud Engineering.
